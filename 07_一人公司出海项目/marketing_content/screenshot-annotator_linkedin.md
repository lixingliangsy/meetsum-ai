## Article Title
Stop Writing Long Text Descriptions for UI Feedback (Do This Instead)

## Article Body (500-800 words)
If you work in tech, you’ve probably been in this situation: you find a bug in your product’s UI, and you spend 10 minutes writing a detailed text description of the issue: "The login button is misaligned by 5px on the right side of the screen, and the text is cut off when the browser window is resized to 1280px width." Then you send it to your developer, and they still ask you for a screenshot because they can’t reproduce the issue.

Or maybe you’re a designer giving feedback on a mockup: you write a paragraph explaining that the spacing between the header and the body text is too large, only for the designer to misread your feedback and adjust the wrong spacing.

Long text descriptions for visual feedback are inefficient, time-consuming, and prone to miscommunication. That’s why we built Screenshot Annotator: a simple tool that lets you take a screenshot, add annotations in seconds, and share it instantly. No more long text descriptions — just clear, visual feedback that everyone can understand immediately.

Here’s how it works: press a hotkey to capture your screen (full screen, window, or custom region), open the annotation editor, add arrows, text boxes, highlights, and shapes to mark up the screenshot, blur sensitive information if needed, and save or share it in one click. It works on all major operating systems, and we have a browser extension so you can capture and annotate web pages without leaving your browser.

Our users save an average of 15 minutes per feedback session compared to writing text descriptions. One product manager at a SaaS company told us that using Screenshot Annotator reduced the number of back-and-forth messages between her team and developers by 60%, because the visual feedback was clear and unambiguous.
